Gentle Movement When You're Stressed

By Sarah M. in Movement · 2 Comments

When you're stressed, your body holds tension in your shoulders, neck, jaw, and back. Sitting for long periods makes it worse. You don't need a gym or an hour of exercise—a few minutes of gentle movement can release that tension and help you feel more grounded.

Why Movement Helps

Stress activates the sympathetic nervous system (fight or flight). Gentle movement and stretching encourage the parasympathetic system (rest and digest). It also increases blood flow, loosens tight muscles, and gives your mind a break from rumination. You're not trying to get a workout in; you're giving your body a chance to reset.

Simple Moves You Can Do Anywhere

Neck and shoulders. Slowly roll your shoulders back five times, then forward five times. Gently tilt your head to the right (ear toward shoulder), hold for a breath, then left. Avoid forcing; stop if anything hurts.

Upper back. Sit or stand and clasp your hands in front of you. Round your upper back and push your hands forward, feeling a stretch between your shoulder blades. Hold for a few breaths.

Hips and legs. If you've been sitting a long time, stand up and do a few slow squats or leg swings. A short walk—even around the room or block—can ease stiffness and clear your head.

When to Do It

You don't need a set time. Try a few stretches when you feel tension building, after a long meeting, or when you're stuck on a problem. Even two or three minutes can make a difference. The key is gentle and consistent—listen to your body and don't push into pain.
